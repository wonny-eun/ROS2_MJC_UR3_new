Why positions can stay “almost” aligned but never identical
  • MJCF vs URDF kinematics: The scene wrapper states the MJCF (UR3_RG2.xml) uses RG2 CAD / non-official DH, while MoveIt / robot_state_publisher use ur_description. Joint angles are shared, but link frames differ → FK of tool0 (URDF) and the kinematic chain to Case_Base (MJCF bodies) systematically disagree slightly.
  • Root alignment: URDF mounts base_link under world at xyz="0 0 0.78" rpy="0 0 -π/2" to match the sim; residual error can come from MJCF solver drift, contact, or timestep.
  • Frame conventions: MuJoCo body frames and URDF link frames use different conventions (+Z mesh offset on gripper CAD, quaternion on Case_Base geom, etc.). Orientation misalignment often exceeds translation error.
  • Weld equality: After Weld_Module_1_To_Case_Base, the plate is snapped in MJCF with a commanded offset; ROS IK still targets URDF TCP, not the post-weld MJCF equilibrium.
  • Trajectory execution / timing: Snapshots use TF at slightly different solver steps than MV.
